What is a Mimic?

Mimics are fascinating creatures in the Dungeons and Dragons mythos.
Dungeons and Dragons, more commonly referred to as "DND" or "D&D"
is a fantasy role-playing game first created in 1974.
In D&D, a very infamous monster for the players to face against is the Mimic.
Mimics are known for hiding in plain sight.
They are named after what they do, which is to imitate some non-living, inanimate object.
The typical form they take is that of a chest. With large teeth suprising the players

History of Mimics:

The Mimics first appeared in 1977's Monster Manual by Gary Gygax
Gygax was one of the co-founders of the game of D&D itself.
The depiction of a Mimic has changed a lot as can be seen from the first depiction below.

In 1983, Ed Greenwood produced a more coherent knowledge base for mimic history.
He created the idea that mimics consisted of two types:
Small neutral mimics that might help the players and Large violent mimics with little intelligence

In 1995, Discworld was released, which introduced a helpful companion called Luggage
Luggage acted as the player's pet, which was a large change from other iterations of the mimic.
